# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect everyone who participates in the Hogo community to adhere to this Code of Conduct in all interactions and spaces managed by the Hogo project, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories, issues, and pull requests
- Project discussions and forums
- Social media interactions
- Community events and meetings

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful**: Listen actively and engage with diverse perspectives
- **Being inclusive**: Welcome and support newcomers and people of all backgrounds
- **Being constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and focus on solutions
- **Being professional**: Maintain a high standard of discourse and documentation
- **Being patient**: Recognize that people have different experience levels and communication styles
- **Being honest**: Acknowledge mistakes and learn from them

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, bullying, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Actions based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics
- **Abuse**: Personal attacks, insults, or inflammatory language
- **Spam**: Excessive promotion, off-topic content, or unsolicited commercial activity
- **Trolling**: Deliberately disruptive behavior intended to provoke conflict
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Unwelcome advances**: Sexual or romantic advances without explicit consent

## Reporting & Enforcement

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Document the incident** with as much detail as possible (date, time, participants, context)
2. **Report privately** by emailing the project maintainers
3. **Remain confidential** - we will keep reports confidential and will not disclose the reporter's identity without consent

The Hogo project maintainers are responsible for:

- Investigating all reports promptly and fairly
- Taking appropriate action to address violations
- Protecting the confidentiality of reporters
- Updating the community on outcomes when appropriate

Violations may result in:

- Warning or caution
- Temporary or permanent ban from project spaces
- Escalation to appropriate authorities if necessary

## Leadership Responsibility

Project leaders are expected to model the values outlined in this Code of Conduct and actively work to foster an inclusive, welcoming community. This includes:

- Addressing violations quickly and fairly
- Providing clear guidance on expected behavior
- Creating safe spaces for people to report issues
- Continuously improving community practices
